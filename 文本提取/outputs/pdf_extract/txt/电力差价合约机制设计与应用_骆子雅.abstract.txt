In the environment of electricity spot market, the
spot price based on time and location will bring the risk of
price fluctuation, so it is necessary to design effective tools to
avoid the risks. As the most common financial instrument in
medium- and long-term market, contract-for-difference (CfD)
can provide market participants with an instrument to hedge
against price fluctuations, and be used by government to
achieve stranded cost recovery, market power control, and
renewable energy accommodation. This paper firstly presents
basic concepts related to CfD design, including contract body,
contract period and contract design parameters. Then, based on
relevant experience in China and abroad, the design elements
and methods of CfDs are summarized, and the selection
methods of design parameters such as contract quantity,
contract price and benchmark price are discussed. Then, this
paper presents design of typical CfDs in the United Kingdom,
Canada, Singapore, Australia and other countries, and discusses
and analyzes their application. Finally, taking the current status
of electricity market in China into consideration, this paper
proposes implications and suggestions for design of CfD
mechanism in construction of electricity market in China.
